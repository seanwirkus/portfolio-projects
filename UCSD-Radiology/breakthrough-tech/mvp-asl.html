MVP-ASL: Advanced MRI of Cerebral Blood Flow

Unlocking the future of brain health through MRI technology.
MVP-ASL (MicroVascular Pulsatility and Velocity-Selective Arterial Spin Labeling) is a breakthrough technique that captures something conventional imaging simply can’t: the subtle rhythmic pulse of blood coursing through the brain’s tiniest vessels.

By pairing velocity-selective arterial spin labeling with retrospective cardiac gating, MVP-ASL synchronizes every image frame to the patient’s heartbeat. The result is a dynamic, beat-by-beat map of cerebral blood flow and microvascular pulsatility—an early, quantitative marker of cerebrovascular health.
Animation illustrating how MVP-ASL visualizes the brain's blood flow pulsing with each heartbeat.
Higher blood flow is shown in red, while lower flow is depicted in blue.
Transitions between high and low flow occur dynamically throughout the cardiac cycle.

The MVP-ASL Approach

Beat-by-beat mapping of the brain’s microvascular pulse
This figure illustrates how MVP-ASL—MicroVascular Pulsatility with Velocity-Selective Arterial Spin Labeling—combines velocity-selective labeling with retrospective cardiac gating so that MRI can capture a complete perfusion map on every heartbeat.

By synchronizing each image frame to the cardiac cycle, the sequence quantifies MicroVascular Pulsatility (MVP), a beat-level waveform of blood flow in arterioles and capillaries that standard MRI cannot resolve.

This beat-by-beat view enables earlier detection of microvascular dysfunction, delivers dynamic quantitative biomarkers for monitoring stroke, dementia, and small-vessel therapies, and provides a potential early indicator of neurodegenerative risk—linking subtle vascular changes to conditions such as Alzheimer’s disease.

Core Parameters

Velocity-selective labeling: $v_c = 2$ cm/s cutoff threshold

Temporal Resolution: $\approx 30$ ms per cardiac phase

Spatial Resolution: 2–3 mm isotropic voxels

Signal Equations

MVP-ASL Signal:

$$S(t) = S_0 \left(1 - 2\alpha \cdot e^{-TI/T_1} \cdot CBF(t)\right)$$

Variables:
$S_0$ = equilibrium magnetization
$\alpha$ = labeling efficiency
$TI$ = post-labeling delay
$T_1$ = longitudinal relaxation time
$CBF(t)$ = time-varying cerebral blood flow

Quantification Methods

Pulsatility Index (PI):

$$PI = \frac{CBF_{max} - CBF_{min}}{CBF_{mean}}$$

Temporal Features:
• Time-to-peak (TTP)
• Full-width at half-maximum (FWHM)
• Mean transit time (MTT)

Validation Metrics

Signal Quality:
SNR $> 15$ in gray matter
CV $< 5\%$ temporal stability

Reliability:
ICC $> 0.85$ spatial reproducibility
$> 90\%$ test-retest reliability
